# Popcorn Perfection (Minimal Oil)

A guide to fast, consistent popcorn with minimal oil, no additives, and good crunch. Popcorn pops when the internal moisture (around 13-14%) flashes to steam and ruptures the hull. Reluctant popping is almost always down to heat transfer, not kernel age.

## Stovetop (best flavour, least oil, most control)

### You need

- Heavy pan with a lid (cast iron or thick stainless ideal)
- ½-1 tsp high-smoke-point oil (rapeseed, sunflower, peanut)
- 2-3 tbsp popcorn kernels
- Salt, to finish

### Method

1. Preheat the pan over medium-high heat, add the oil, drop in 2-3 test kernels and put the lid on.
2. Wait until the test kernels pop. This guarantees the pan is hot enough and is the key step most people skip.
3. Add the remaining kernels, swirl to coat lightly, and put the lid back on immediately.
4. Once popping starts, shake every few seconds to keep the kernels moving and prevent scorching.
5. Remove from the heat when popping slows to one or two seconds between pops; do not wait for silence.
6. Take the lid off immediately so steam does not soften the popcorn.
7. Salt after popping.

## Microwave (no oil possible, very consistent)

### You need

- Plain popcorn kernels
- Microwave-safe bowl
- Vented lid or plate

### Method

1. Add 2-3 tbsp kernels to the bowl.
2. Cover loosely so steam can vent.
3. Microwave on full power.
4. Stop when pops slow to one or two seconds apart.
5. Salt after popping. Tossing with ½ tsp neutral oil afterwards helps the salt stick.

## Why the air fryer struggles

Kernels need conductive heat, but air fryers rely on convection. The kernels overheat on the outside before internal steam pressure builds, giving scorched kernels and a poor popping rate. It is the wrong tool for the job.

## Getting salt to stick (no additives)

- Grind the salt very fine, almost to a powder.
- Or dissolve salt in a few drops of hot water and mist the popcorn lightly.
- Or add salt to the oil after popping and toss quickly.
- Salt after popping, not before; salt pre-pop interferes with moisture release.

## If popping is still slow

Try rehydrating the kernels: add 1 tsp water to ½ cup kernels, seal overnight, shaking occasionally, then pop the next day. If popping improves, the kernels were too dry.
